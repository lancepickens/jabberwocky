# Current Leading Fusion Projects and Their Targets

## ITER (International Tokamak)
- **Status**: Construction ongoing, major delays
- **First plasma**: Now expected 2033-2034 (originally 2020, then 2025)
- **D-D operations**: 2036
- **D-T operations**: 2039
- **Q target**: Q >= 10 (500 MW from 50 MW input)
- **Purpose**: Experimental, NOT a power plant
- **Budget**: Billions over original estimates
- **Key issues**: Vacuum vessel geometric non-conformities, thermal shield corrosion, nuclear safety authority concerns

## Commonwealth Fusion Systems (SPARC)
- **Type**: Compact tokamak with high-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnets
- **Status**: Under construction; near completion late 2026, first plasma 2027
- **Q target**: Q > 10 (50-100 MW fusion power)
- **Commercial plant (ARC)**: Early 2030s, Virginia; 400 MW to grid (~300,000 homes)
- **Funding**: >$1 billion raised; backed by Breakthrough Energy Ventures, others
- **Partners**: Siemens, Nvidia for AI-assisted design

## Helion Energy
- **Type**: Pulsed field-reversed configuration (FRC); direct electricity conversion
- **Status**: Broke ground on Orion (first commercial facility) July 2025, Malaga, WA
- **Target**: Electricity production by 2028; 50 MW to Microsoft by 2029
- **Funding**: $425M Series F (Jan 2025); total >$1B
- **Unique approach**: Direct energy conversion (no steam turbine), uses D-He3 fuel

## TAE Technologies
- **Type**: Field-reversed configuration, hydrogen-boron (p-B11) fuel
- **Status**: Norman device testing; Copernicus under construction (operations ~2025)
- **Commercial plant (Da Vinci)**: Early 2030s
- **Funding**: >$1 billion (now public via SPAC)
- **Unique**: Aneutronic fuel = minimal radioactive waste

## Zap Energy
- **Type**: Sheared-flow-stabilized Z-pinch
- **Status**: R&D ongoing
- **Target**: Aims for compact, low-cost fusion
- **Advantage**: No magnets or lasers needed

## First Light Fusion (UK)
- **Type**: Projectile-based inertial confinement
- **Status**: Limited public updates in 2025-2026
- **Approach**: Hypervelocity projectile compresses fuel

## Pacific Fusion
- **Type**: Laser-based ICF
- **Status**: Emerged from stealth Nov 2024 with $900M Series A
- **Approach**: Commercializing NIF-style laser fusion

## NIF/LLNL (National Ignition Facility)
- **Type**: ICF (research facility, not power plant)
- **Status**: Active research; Q=4.13 achieved April 2025
- **Future**: Laser upgrades to 2.6 MJ (2025-2027) and 3 MJ (~2030)
- **Projections**: Q=5-8 at 2.6 MJ; Q>10 at 3 MJ
