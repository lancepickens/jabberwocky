# Fusion Energy Funding and Policy Landscape (2024-2026)

## Overall Investment Scale

- **Annual investment (2024-2025)**: $2.64 billion in 12 months to July 2025 (FIA report)
- **Cumulative global investment**: ~$15.1 billion (as of Sept 2025, up 30% from June)
- **Private investment**: >$10 billion globally
- **Public funding growth**: Up 84% from 2024, nearly $800 million total
- **US Congressional fusion appropriation**: ~$760 million (FY2023)

## US Government Programs

- **FIRE Collaboratives**: $128M initial ($220M over 4 years)
- **INFUSE Program**: $6.1M for 20 public-private projects
- **Milestone Program**: Cost-sharing model (private >50%)
- **Office of Fusion**: New DOE office for commercialization
- **Fusion S&T Roadmap**: Released Oct 2025; targets power on grid by mid-2030s

## Global Competition

- **China**: $6.5 billion spent 2023-2025 alone (>3x US spending); 71.2% state-led
- **US**: 94.5% private capital led
- **EU/UK/Germany/South Korea**: $9 billion collectively
- **EU model**: Hybrid (33.9% public, 64% private, 2.1% PPP)

## Industry Survey Data (FIA 2025)

- 53 fusion companies surveyed (up from 23 in 2021)
- 8 new entrants since 2024
- 84% believe fusion electricity on grid before end of 2030s
- 53% believe by 2035
- 83% consider insufficient investment a major challenge
- Median additional funding needed per company: $700M
- Range: $3M to $12.5B

## Investor Types

- Deep tech VC: DCVC, Breakthrough Energy Ventures
- Industrial: Chevron, Siemens Energy, Nucor
- Sovereign/quasi-public: In-Q-Tel, European Innovation Council
- Energy sector: Shell Ventures, Energy Impact Partners

## Societal Value Estimate

- MIT Energy Initiative (2024): Fusion could increase societal value by $3.6-8.7 trillion depending on construction costs
