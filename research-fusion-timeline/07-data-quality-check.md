# Data Quality Assessment: Fusion Q Factor Claims and Timeline Predictions

## Q Factor Data Verification

### Verified (High Confidence)

| Claim | Source Quality | Verification |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| JET Q=0.67 (1997) | Peer-reviewed, independently confirmed | VERIFIED. Published in Nuclear Fusion journal. 16 MW from 24 MW input. |
| TFTR Q=0.28 (1994) | Peer-reviewed | VERIFIED. 10.7 MW D-T fusion power. |
| NIF Q=1.54 (Dec 2022) | Peer-reviewed by 5 independent teams | VERIFIED. Published in Physical Review Letters (2024). |
| NIF Q=4.13 (Apr 2025) | LLNL official announcement, multiple sources | VERIFIED. 8.6 MJ from 2.08 MJ. Consistent across sources. |
| NIF Q=2.44 (Feb 2025) | LLNL official | VERIFIED. 5.0 MJ from 2.05 MJ. |

### Verified with Caveats

| Claim | Issue |
|-------|-------|
| JT-60 Q=1.25 | This is Q_DT_equivalent EXTRAPOLATED from D-D experiments. NOT actual D-T fusion. Often misleadingly cited as "breakeven achieved." Should always be labeled as extrapolated. |
| NIF "achieved breakeven" | True for Q_sci, but extremely misleading without context. Q_eng ~0.01. Many media reports omit this critical distinction. |

### Unverified / Uncertain

| Claim | Issue |
|-------|-------|
| Early tokamak Q values (T-3, PLT) | Specific Q numbers are approximate; these devices predated standardized Q measurements. Q << 0.01 is directionally correct. |
| SPARC Q>10 prediction | Based entirely on simulations and extrapolations. No experimental validation yet. CFS is credible but this is a prediction, not data. |
| NIF wall-plug energy (300-400 MJ) | Multiple sources cite different numbers (300 MJ, 400 MJ, 422 MJ). The exact number depends on what you include. The order of magnitude is correct. |

## Company Timeline Claims Assessment

### Commonwealth Fusion Systems
- **Claim**: First plasma late 2025 -> Revised to 2027
- **Assessment**: Already slipped ~2 years. Pattern consistent with fusion project delays. Q>10 prediction is plausible based on physics but unproven.
- **Reliability**: MODERATE. Best-funded private company, strong MIT pedigree, HTS magnets demonstrated. But timeline optimism is standard in the industry.

### Helion
- **Claim**: Electricity by 2028, 50 MW to Microsoft by 2029
- **Assessment**: HIGHLY OPTIMISTIC. No public Q factor data. No demonstrated net energy gain. Direct conversion is unproven. PPA with Microsoft has escape clauses.
- **Reliability**: LOW for stated timeline. The technology approach is interesting but the gap between current demonstrated performance and claimed near-term goals is enormous.

### TAE Technologies
- **Claim**: Net energy later this decade, Da Vinci commercial plant early 2030s
- **Assessment**: p-B11 fuel requires ~10x higher temperatures than D-T. No demonstrated path to ignition.
- **Reliability**: LOW. The physics challenges of p-B11 fusion are substantially harder than D-T.

### ITER
- **Claim**: Q>=10, D-T operations 2039
- **Assessment**: Credible physics basis (well-understood tokamak scaling laws). Timeline has repeatedly slipped (original: 2016 first plasma, now 2033-2034).
- **Reliability**: MODERATE for physics goals. LOW for schedule (history of delays). But ITER has the most rigorous physics basis for its Q predictions.

## Assessment of the "2030 Breakeven" Question

### What "breakeven for power generation" actually requires:
The question asks about "break-even fusion for power generation" — this means Q_eng > 1 with actual electricity output. This is a MUCH higher bar than Q_sci > 1.

### Data-backed assessment:
1. **Q_sci > 1 already achieved** (NIF, 2022). This milestone is done.
2. **Q_sci > 10 in magnetic confinement**: Possible by 2028-2030 if SPARC succeeds. Probability: 25-40%.
3. **Q_eng > 1 (net electricity from fusion)**: No pathway to achieve this by 2030. The engineering challenges (tritium breeding, materials, heat extraction, plant integration) each require 5-15+ years beyond scientific proof.
4. **Commercial power generation**: 2040s at the earliest, more likely 2050s.

### Reliability of Industry Timeline Predictions
Historical data on fusion timeline predictions shows systematic optimism:
- ITER: 17+ years behind original schedule
- Every FIA survey shows companies claiming 10-15 year timelines
- No private fusion company has ever met its stated timeline for a major milestone

### Key Data Gaps
1. No public Q factor data from Helion, TAE, Zap Energy, or other private companies
2. Exact wall-plug energy for NIF shots varies by source
3. SPARC performance predictions are simulation-based only
4. Tritium breeding ratio data is essentially non-existent (no device has tested this)

## Overall Data Quality Rating

- **Historical Q factor data (tokamaks)**: HIGH quality. Peer-reviewed, independently verified.
- **NIF Q factor data**: HIGH quality. Peer-reviewed, officially reported.
- **Company timeline claims**: LOW quality. Self-reported, systematically optimistic, often unverifiable.
- **Engineering breakeven projections**: SPECULATIVE. No empirical basis for any specific date.
- **Funding data**: MODERATE quality. FIA survey data is self-reported but broadly consistent across sources.
