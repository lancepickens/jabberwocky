What is the Q Factor?


Q = Fusion Energy Out / Heating Energy In

Q ValueMeaning
Q < 1More energy in than out (net energy loss)
Q = 1"Scientific breakeven" — energy out equals energy in
Q = 5Self-heating begins to dominate
Q = 10ITER's target; burning plasma regime
Q ~ 22+Estimated minimum for commercial viability

The Engineering Chasm

Five unsolved problems between Q>1 and power generation


1. Tritium fuel — Global supply: <4 kg/yr. A plant needs ~56 kg/yr. Breeding blankets untested.

2. Materials — No material survives years of 14 MeV neutron bombardment. Qualification takes 15-25 years.

3. Continuous operation — NIF fires for nanoseconds. Best sustained: 18 min (EAST, low Q). Plants need years.

4. Electricity generation — No fusion device has ever generated a single watt of electricity.

5. Systems integration — Fuel cycle, heat extraction, power conditioning, remote maintenance — all unbuilt.

The Nuanced View


Real Progress

  • Qsci jumped from 0.7 to 4.13 in 4 years
  • HTS magnets proven at scale
  • 53 companies, $15B+ invested
  • DOE Fusion Roadmap released
  • Multiple approaches being pursued

Hard Truths

  • No fusion device has generated electricity
  • Qeng is ~100x below breakeven
  • Tokamak Q stagnant since 1997
  • Tritium, materials, breeding all unsolved
  • Every timeline prediction has been late

Fusion is no longer "always 30 years away."
It is probably 10–20 years from first electricity and 15–25 years from commercial significance.

Key Takeaway


Scientific fusion milestones will continue to be achieved through 2030.
Higher Q values are coming.


But scientific breakeven and power generation breakeven
are separated by an enormous engineering chasm.


The Q factor record will keep climbing.
Your lights won't run on fusion before 2030.
